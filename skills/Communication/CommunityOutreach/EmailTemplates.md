# Email Templates

Outreach tone guidelines, structure, anti-patterns, and examples for generating authentic, personalized outreach emails.

**Every email is a draft. Never auto-send. Jm reviews and personalizes before sending.**

---

## Core Principles

1. **Specificity over flattery.** "I read your post on agent architectures and your point about tool-use patterns resonated" beats "I'm impressed by your work."
2. **Peer-to-peer, not fan-to-creator.** Position Jm as a fellow traveler, not an admirer requesting time.
3. **One clear ask.** Coffee, virtual chat, attend the same event. Never multiple asks.
4. **Under 150 words.** Respect their time. Long emails signal sales pitch.
5. **No follow-up pressure.** One polite follow-up after 7 days. Then silence. Respect is more memorable than persistence.
6. **Reference something recent.** Their latest post, talk, project. Shows genuine attention, not mass outreach.
7. **Include context on Jm.** One sentence on what Jm does/builds so they can assess fit. Link to blog or project if relevant.
8. **Warm over cold.** If there is any shared connection, event, or community -- lead with that.

## Tone Profiles

### casual-friendly
**Use for:** Surf, volleyball, music, hobby-based communities
**Voice:** Relaxed, genuine, like messaging someone you met briefly at an event
**Avoid:** Corporate language, bullet points, formal greetings

**Example opening:** "Hey [Name] -- saw you at the [event/group] and wanted to reach out."

### professional
**Use for:** AI/tech community, potential cofounders, career connections
**Voice:** Direct, peer-level, substance-focused. Not corporate, not salesy.
**Avoid:** Buzzwords, jargon-stacking, "picking your brain"

**Example opening:** "Hi [Name], I came across your work on [specific project/talk] and it resonated with what I'm building."

### community-peer
**Use for:** Writing groups, DSA, local community organizations
**Voice:** Fellow member, interested in the community, not just networking
**Avoid:** Transactional language, asking for favors before establishing rapport

**Example opening:** "Hi [Name], I'm getting more involved in [community] and noticed your [contribution/role]."

## Email Structure

```
Subject: [Specific reference - NOT generic]

[1-2 sentences: How Jm found them + specific reference to their work]

[1 sentence: What Jm does/builds that's relevant]

[1 sentence: The specific, low-friction ask]

[Sign-off]
Jm
```

---

## Subject Line Guidelines

**Good subjects:**
- "Fellow SD AI builder -- loved your talk on agents"
- "Your fiction workshop at [venue] -- fellow writer here"
- "San Diego surf community -- new to the scene"
- "Re: [event name] -- connecting after the meetup"

**Bad subjects:**
- "Networking opportunity"
- "Quick question"
- "Can I pick your brain?"
- "Introduction"
- "[Company name] partnership"

---

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER USE)

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Alternative |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| "I'm a huge fan of your work" | Fan positioning, not peer | "Your point about X resonated because..." |
| "I'd love to pick your brain" | Extractive framing | "I'm working on something similar and would enjoy comparing notes" |
| "I know you're busy, but..." | Self-deprecating opener | Just make the ask directly |
| "I have an amazing opportunity" | Sales pitch signal | Describe what you're building naturally |
| "Let me know when works for you" | Too open-ended | "Would you be up for coffee next week? I'm flexible Tue-Thu afternoons" |
| Multiple paragraphs about Jm | Self-centered | One sentence about Jm, focus on the connection |
| "Hope this email finds you well" | Generic, automated feel | Skip pleasantries, lead with substance |
| Including a resume or portfolio | Too formal, interview-like | Brief mention of relevant work, link if natural |
| "I noticed we have a lot in common" | Vague, could be anyone | Name the specific things in common |
| Follow-up: "Just bumping this up" | Aggressive, pressuring | One gentle follow-up referencing original, then stop |

## Follow-Up Template

**Timing:** Exactly 7 days after initial email
**Maximum:** One follow-up per contact, ever

```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hey [Name], just wanted to float this back up -- no pressure at all.
[One brief reminder of the ask or add something new: "Also saw you'll be at [event]"].

Either way, keep up the great work on [specific thing].

Jm
```

## Quality Checklist

Before creating a Gmail draft, verify:

- [ ] Under 150 words
- [ ] References something specific about the recipient
- [ ] One clear, low-friction ask
- [ ] Peer-to-peer tone (not fan-to-creator)
- [ ] No anti-patterns used
- [ ] Subject line is specific, not generic
- [ ] Includes brief context on Jm (1 sentence max)
- [ ] Appropriate tone for the community
- [ ] Created as DRAFT only
