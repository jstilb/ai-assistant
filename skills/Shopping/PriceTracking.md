# Price Tracking SOP

Standard operating procedure for tracking prices and identifying optimal purchase timing.

---

## When to Track Prices

- Items over $100 that aren't urgent
- Products with known sale cycles
- User explicitly asks "when should I buy"

---

## Price Research Methods

### Web Search Approach
Use WebSearch to find:
1. **Current prices** across retailers
2. **Price history** discussions/reviews
3. **Upcoming sales** announcements
4. **Coupon codes** if available

### Key Search Queries
- `[product name] price history`
- `[product name] sale [current year]`
- `[retailer] upcoming sales [month]`
- `best time to buy [product category]`

---

## Sale Cycle Knowledge

### Seasonal Patterns
| Category | Best Time to Buy | Typical Discount |
|----------|------------------|------------------|
| Winter gear | Feb-March | 30-50% |
| Summer gear | Aug-Sept | 30-40% |
| Electronics | Black Friday, Prime Day | 20-40% |
| Outdoor | REI Anniversary Sale (May) | 20-30% |

### Event-Based Sales
| Event | When | Best For |
|-------|------|----------|
| Black Friday/Cyber Monday | Late November | Electronics, general goods |
| Prime Day | July | Amazon ecosystem, tech |
| REI Member Sales | Quarterly | Outdoor gear |
| End of Season | Feb (winter), Aug (summer) | Seasonal clothing |

---

## Tracking Workflow

1. **Identify** target product and current price
2. **Research** historical prices via web search
3. **Check** upcoming sale events
4. **Calculate** wait value: expected savings vs immediate need
5. **Recommend** with confidence level:
   - **Buy now** - Price is at/near historical low
   - **Wait** - Sale event within 4 weeks
   - **Set alert** - Good price but might drop further
